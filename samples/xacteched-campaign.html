The Challenge

Xactware faced a significant talent pipeline problem. The company needed skilled developers familiar with construction and insurance estimation software, but candidates with this specialized knowledge were rare. Traditional recruiting wasn't working—we needed to build our own talent pipeline.

I identified an opportunity to partner with Utah's technical education system to create a custom training program that would prepare students for careers at Xactware while addressing the state's workforce development goals.

The Solution

I conceived and built the XactechED program from the ground up—branding, curriculum, partnerships, and funding strategy. The program was designed to:

  • Teach web development fundamentals aligned with Xactware's technology stack
  • Provide real-world project experience using actual company tools
  • Create a direct employment pathway for program graduates
  • Scale through partnership with Mountainland Technical College (MTECH)

Securing the Grant

The Utah Cluster Acceleration Partnership (UCAP) offered funding for workforce development initiatives, but competition was fierce. I prepared and delivered a comprehensive presentation to the governor's committee, demonstrating:

  • Clear ROI for state investment through job creation metrics
  • Sustainable program model that could scale beyond initial funding
  • Industry partnership commitment from Xactware leadership
  • Curriculum alignment with state technical education standards

The presentation secured a $200,000 grant—funding that enabled program launch and first-year operations.

Results & Impact

The XactechED program demonstrated what's possible when corporate training needs align with public workforce development goals:

  • Established formal partnership between Xactware and MTECH
  • Created proprietary curriculum that could be replicated
  • Built a sustainable talent pipeline for technical roles
  • Received press coverage in local business publications
